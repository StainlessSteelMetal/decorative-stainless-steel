They combine:

Stainless steel base material
Vacuum-coated metallic color layer
Optional textured surface (mirror, brushed, ripple, etched)

These materials are widely used in commercial buildings, luxury interiors, and architectural projects.

3. Technical Advantages of PVD Coating
3.1 Physical Structure

PVD coating is applied in a vacuum environment where metal atoms are deposited onto stainless steel surfaces.

This results in:

Strong adhesion layer
High surface hardness
Uniform color distribution
3.2 Performance Benefits

Compared to traditional coating methods:

Higher scratch resistance
No fading under normal indoor use
Environmentally friendly process
No toxic chemical waste
3.3 Thickness Range

Standard sheet thickness options:

0.3 mm – 2.0 mm

This allows flexible usage in:

Wall panels
Elevator interiors
Furniture surfaces
Architectural cladding

6. Technical FAQ
Q1: What is the lifespan of PVD coating?

Typically more than 10 years under indoor usage conditions.

Q2: Can PVD stainless steel be customized?

Yes. Color, texture, and thickness can all be customized.

Q3: Is it suitable for outdoor use?

Yes, but 316-grade stainless steel is recommended for harsh environments.

Q4: What is the most popular finish?

Water ripple and mirror finishes are currently the most widely used.
